Hello & Welcome

We're getting married!

We absolutely can't wait to celebrate our wedding with all of our friends and family, over the long weekend in August.

Our ceremony will be on Saturday at Canterbury Cathedral and our reception will be on both Saturday and Sunday at Leeds Castle, which is definitely not in Leeds.

We really hope you can join us to make the weekend extremely special.

Wedding Events

The Weekend

The ceremony will be in the Crypt of Canterbury Cathedral, followed by drinks and (substantial) canapés in the Chapter House. Guests will then be transported to Leeds Castle for the rest of the weekend. There will be a Chinese tea ceremony at the castle, followed by drinks, dinner, and dancing. Sunday will be the most epic of game days, with a BBQ, formal dinner, and diversions in the castle. Rooms are booked through to Monday morning. We know some of you are coming from far away, so to make a full event of it we are also organising a day of punting in Cambridge on Tuesday Aug 29th; we’d love for you to stay and hang out with us.

Important Information

If you are not driving, please bring your luggage to the cathedral before the ceremony, tag it, and put it in the luggage van. It will be taken to the castle and put into your room in advance of your arrival. If you need access to something (e.g. medication, baby formula) between the ceremony and the castle, please keep it with you.

No stiletto heels are allowed at any of the venues due to the very old floors.

There is no phone signal in Canterbury. Like, at all. So please download any information you need (tickets, directions, maps, this website) before you get to Canterbury.

There is no parking at the cathedral, so plan to park nearby and walk a short distance. There is parking at the castle.

Breakfast is included on both Sunday and Monday mornings.

Check out is 9:45am sharp on Monday (£1,000 fine per late room).

Travel Information



Canterbury

BY PLANE

Heathrow, Gatwick, and Stansted airports all have easy and direct trains into London. Luton airport sucks; don't do it. Download the Citymapper app to get accurate train times. You can use contactless payment (i.e. Apple pay, Google wallet, contactless cards) on all London airport trains, so you don't need to buy a ticket. Tickets can be purchased at all airport train stations if needed. There are no direct trains from airports to Canterbury, you need to go via London. Let us know if you get to town a few days early!

BY TRAIN

Trains to Canterbury West leave from St. Pancras station (connected to Heathrow airport), and Stratford International.
Trains to Canterbury East leave from London Victoria station (connected to Gatwick airport).
Both Canterbury stations are a 10 minute walk to the cathedral. Or, you can book a taxi for a 3-5 minute drive, they accept card payment. https://canterburytaxis.co.uk/ https://cabcocanterbury.co.uk/

BY CAR

Put CT1 2PY into your GPS and drive there. You will lose signal when you get close. Download your map.
You can't park at the cathedral. The nearest parking structure is the Castle Street lot, which is a 7 minute walk to the cathedral.
Castle Street Car Park, 40A Castle St, Canterbury CT1 2PY

BY BOAT

Attach your boat to a trailer. See 'by car'.


Leeds Castle

BY BUS

We will provide transportation for you and your luggage (unless you're driving) from the cathedral to the castle. PLEASE DROP YOUR LUGGAGE OFF AT THE LUGGAGE VAN INSIDE THE CATHEDRAL GATES BEFORE THE CEREMONY. Your luggage will be driven to the castle and put into your rooms. If you need access to anything before 5pm (like medication) please keep it with you.
You will be ferried from reception to reception in a red, double-decker, vintage Routemaster bus.


From Leeds Castle

After checking out promptly at 9:45am, you are welcome to stay in the grounds at Leeds Castle at your leisure. There are cafes, gardens, playgrounds, wildlife, and a golf course to enjoy. When you’re ready to leave, Maidstone train station is a short taxi ride from Leeds Castle. There are direct trains to Charing Cross and London Victoria stations. Perhaps you've made friends during the weekend and you can share a ride :)

If you drove there, drive home (safely).


Cambridge

Trains to Cambridge leave from King's Cross and Liverpool St. stations. If you want to see platform 9 ¾ go to King's Cross early. There's a long line. You can buy tickets at the station or online at https://www.nationalrail.co.uk/
