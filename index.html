Important Information

If you are not driving, please bring your luggage to the cathedral before the ceremony, tag it, and put it in the luggage van. It will be taken to the castle and put into your room in advance of your arrival. If you need access to something (e.g. medication, baby formula) between the ceremony and the castle, please keep it with you.

No stiletto heels are allowed at any of the venues due to the very old floors.

There is no phone signal in Canterbury. Like, at all. So please download any information you need (tickets, directions, maps, this website) before you get to Canterbury.

There is no parking at the cathedral, so plan to park nearby and walk a short distance. There is parking at the castle.

Breakfast is included on both Sunday and Monday mornings.

You will need to check in when you arrive at the castle in order to collect room keys.

Leeds Castle is a cashless venue, contactless card/phone payments only.

Check out is 9:45am sharp on Monday (£1,000 fine per late room).

Travel Information



Canterbury

BY PLANE

Heathrow, Gatwick, and Stansted airports all have easy and direct trains into London. Luton airport sucks; don't do it. Download the Citymapper app to get accurate train times. You can use contactless payment (i.e. Apple pay, Google wallet, contactless cards) to get Heathrow and Gatwick-London airport trains, so you don't need to buy a ticket. Stansted airport train requires purchasing a ticket. Tickets can be purchased at all airport train stations if needed. There are no direct trains from airports to Canterbury, you need to go via London. Let us know if you get to town a few days early!

BY TRAIN

Trains to Canterbury West leave from St. Pancras station (connected to Heathrow airport), and Stratford International.
Trains to Canterbury East leave from London Victoria station (connected to Gatwick airport).
Both Canterbury stations are a 10 minute walk to the cathedral. Or, you can book a taxi for a 3-5 minute drive, they accept card payment. https://canterburytaxis.co.uk/, https://cabcocanterbury.co.uk/

BY CAR

Put CT1 2PY into your GPS and drive there. You will lose signal when you get close. Download your map.
You can't park at the cathedral. The nearest parking structure is the Castle Street lot, which is a 7 minute walk to the cathedral.
Castle Street Car Park, 40A Castle St, Canterbury CT1 2PY

BY BOAT

Attach your boat to a trailer. See 'by car'.


Leeds Castle

WE WILL TAKE YOU BY BUS

We will provide transportation for you and your luggage (unless you're driving) from the cathedral to the castle. PLEASE DROP YOUR LUGGAGE OFF AT THE LUGGAGE VAN INSIDE THE CATHEDRAL GATES BEFORE THE CEREMONY. Your luggage will be driven to the castle and put into your rooms. If you need access to anything before 5pm (like medication) please keep it with you.
You will be ferried from reception to reception in a red, double-decker, vintage Routemaster bus.

BY CAR

After the cathedral reception, drive to Leeds Castle. Here's a good route. There is parking at the castle, go to the Broomfield road entrance and tell them you're a hotel guest.


Leaving Leeds Castle

After checking out promptly at 9:45am, you are welcome to stay in the grounds at Leeds Castle at your leisure. There are cafes, gardens, playgrounds, wildlife, and a golf course to enjoy. When you're ready to leave, Maidstone train station is a short taxi ride from Leeds Castle. There are direct trains to Charing Cross and London Victoria stations. Perhaps you've made friends during the weekend and you can share a lift :)

If you drove there, drive home (safely).


Cambridge

Trains to Cambridge leave from King's Cross and Liverpool St. stations. If you want to see platform 9 ¾ go to King's Cross early. There's a long line. You can buy tickets at the station or online at https://www.nationalrail.co.uk/

On Saturday and Sunday nights we have reserved all the rooms at Leeds Castle. There are rooms in the castle, in the stable courtyard, and at River Lodge. If you need access to a kitchen, fridge, microwave, or washer during your stay, please let us know in your RSVP.

Visit the FAQ page to see things to do at Leeds Castle. Guests are welcome to stay through the day on Monday and have access to the grounds.

Questions!



What shall I wear?

  • Saturday - Semi-formal to formal. Americans - please note that in the UK black is only for funerals!
  • Sunday BBQ - garden party attire (summer cocktail).
  • Sunday evening - Semi-formal, colourful, creative!
  • Hats - ladies' formal hats and fascinators are permitted in the cathedral.
  • Shoes - NO STILETTO HEELS, these are not permitted in the venues due to the very old floors. Wedges and block heels are ok.

I am American, do I need a visa/covid tests to enter the UK?

  • Americans will receive a visa on arrival - see https://www.gov.uk/check-uk-visa/y/usa/tourism for more info.
  • The the official COVID guidance will have the latest requirements for travelling to the UK.

How can I get to the cathedral from the train station?

  • You can walk to the cathedral from Canterbury West in about 12 minutes.
  • You can walk to the cathedral from Canterbury East in about 10 minutes.
  • You can book online or call a taxi from the train stations.
    • https://canterburytaxis.co.uk/ 01227 444 444
    • https://cabcocanterbury.co.uk/ 01227 455 455

What do I do with my luggage?

  • There will be a van at the cathedral parking lot. Please tag your luggage with your name, and put it in the van. The luggage will be driven to the castle. Please arrive early to drop off your luggage.

Can we see Canterbury Cathedral as tourists?

  • Yes, we recommend it! The cathedral opens at 9am and you can buy tickets online or at the gate. If you visit before the wedding, please make your way down to the crypt at 12:15pm and tell the Vergers you're there for our wedding.

What if I can't afford the accommodation?

  • We look forward to celebrating with everyone and understand that costs can be prohibitive.
  • Please get in touch with us to arrange alternatives, we can sort something out.

Can we visit Leeds Castle?

  • Yes, you will get a wristband when you check in. This wristband gives you access to the castle and grounds on both Sunday and Monday. Leeds Castle has loads to do! The wristband gives you access to:
    • The castle
    • The gardens
    • The maze and grotto
    • The falconry displays
    • The children's playground and obstacle course
    • The 'adventure' (mini) golf course for a surcharge of £4
  • In addition, you can book and pay for these activities (including Monday):
    • GoApe
    • Segway
    • Golf - get a discounted rate if you tell the clubhouse you're a wedding guest.

I have children, what do I do?

  • Unfortunately we can't have any kids over the age of 2. If this means you really can't come, please talk to us and we can figure out some kind of creative solution.
  • If they are under 2, then you are welcome to bring them!
  • If you want to bring a babysitter, we can accommodate them at the River Lodge (where there are fridges and kettles etc) but you will need to pay for more accommodation.
